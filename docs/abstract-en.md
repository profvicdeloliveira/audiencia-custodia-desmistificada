This work analyzes the custody hearing from the perspective of police investigation and human rights, seeking to understand its impacts—positive and negative—on the performance of the Civil Police. It begins with a historical and normative contextualization of the institute, with emphasis on its implementation in Brazil. The objective is to demystify the custody hearing as a mere formalization act of arrest or simple instrument of police control, recognizing in it a democratic space for investigative improvement. Methodologically, the research relies on documentary and statistical analysis, interviews with actors in the penal system, and study of correlate legislation, doctrine, and jurisprudence. As a result, it is identified that the custody hearing exercises a strategic role in mediating between the protection of fundamental rights and the effectiveness of criminal prosecution, pointing out institutional best practices and suggesting normative and procedural improvements. The conclusion emphasizes the importance of inter-institutional dialogue and proactive action by the police authority in preserving legality and building a more expeditious, transparent, and effective investigation. Keywords: Police investigation and operations, Public Security, Custody Hearing, Guarantee Judge, Human Rights.
